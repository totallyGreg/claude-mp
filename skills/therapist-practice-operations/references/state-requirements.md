# State Licensing Requirements for Mental Health Therapists

This reference provides detailed requirements for Washington and Florida, plus a framework for researching other states.

## Washington State

### License Types

- **LMHC (Licensed Mental Health Counselor)**
- **LCSW (Licensed Clinical Social Worker)**
- **LMFT (Licensed Marriage and Family Therapist)**
- **Psychologist** (different credential)

Focus below is on LMHC as an example; requirements vary by credential.

### Initial Licensure

- **Education:** Master's degree (minimum) in approved field, with specific coursework
- **Supervised Experience:** 1,000 hours of supervised clinical practice after master's degree
- **Exam:** Pass Washington state licensing exam or NBCC NCMHCE (National Counselor Examination)
- **Application:** Submit to Washington Department of Health

### License Renewal

- **Renewal Period:** Two-year renewal cycle
- **Renewal Deadline:** License expires on your birthday (varies by individual)
- **Cost:** Approximately $200-$250 per renewal (verify current fee)
- **Continuing Education:** 20 contact hours per renewal period (every 2 years)
  - Ethics: Minimum 3 hours
  - Diversity/Cultural Competency: Minimum 2 hours
  - Domestic Violence: Minimum 2 hours
  - Balance across remaining hours

### CE Requirements

- **Total Hours:** 20 per 2-year period (10 hours/year average)
- **Approved Providers:** NBCC, state counseling association (WACA), professional organizations
- **Topics:** No restrictions beyond the required topics above
- **Credits:** Clock hours = contact hours (no self-study credit)
- **Documentation:** Maintain certificates of attendance; report on renewal application

### Application Timeline

1. **12-18 months before expiration:** Check license expiration date
2. **6 months before:** Begin gathering CE documentation; ensure hours are documented
3. **3 months before:** Start CE if not completed; plan any final courses
4. **30 days before:** Submit renewal application with CE certificates
5. **Expiration date:** License renews upon approval (usually within 2-3 weeks of application submission)

### Business Tax Implications

- **State Income Tax:** NO state income tax on business income (major advantage)
- **B&O Tax:** May apply if gross income exceeds certain thresholds; consult Washington Department of Revenue
- **Estimated Quarterly Payments:** Only federal taxes (no state withholding)

### State Board Contact

- **Department of Health, Health Professions and Quality Assurance Division**
- **Website:** doh.wa.gov (search for "mental health counselor")
- **Phone:** Verify current contact on website

### License Verification

Therapists can verify current licenses online through Washington's provider license lookup.

## Florida State

### License Types

- **LMHC (Licensed Mental Health Counselor)**
- **LCSW (Licensed Clinical Social Worker)**
- **LMFT (Licensed Marriage and Family Therapist)**
- **Professional Counselor (Master's-level)**

### Initial Licensure

- **Education:** Master's degree in mental health field with specific coursework
- **Supervised Experience:** 2,000 hours of supervised clinical practice (higher than Washington) within 5 years of graduation
- **Exam:** Pass Florida examination for mental health counselors (NCMHCE accepted)
- **Application:** Submit to Florida Department of Health, Division of Medical Quality Assurance

### License Renewal

- **Renewal Period:** Two-year renewal cycle
- **Renewal Deadline:** Follows a calendar schedule (specific month based on license type); verify your exact date
- **Cost:** Approximately $250-$300 per renewal (verify current fee)
- **Continuing Education:** 20 contact hours per renewal period (same as Washington)
  - Ethics: Minimum 3 hours
  - Supervision (if supervising others): Additional requirements
  - Other: Professional development topics

### CE Requirements

- **Total Hours:** 20 per 2-year period
- **Approved Providers:** American Counseling Association (ACA), Florida Counseling Association, accredited universities, approved providers
- **Topics:** Broad flexibility; ethics required
- **Credits:** Clock hours = contact hours
- **Documentation:** Maintain certificates; report on renewal application

### Application Timeline

1. **18 months before expiration:** Determine exact renewal deadline (varies by credential)
2. **12 months before:** Start planning CE completion
3. **6 months before:** Ensure CE completion; begin renewal application preparation
4. **60 days before:** Submit renewal application with CE certificates
5. **Expiration:** Renew online through Florida DBPR portal

### Business Tax Implications

- **State Income Tax:** NO state income tax on business income (major advantage, same as Washington)
- **Sales Tax:** May apply to certain services (rare for therapists, but verify if billing for workshops/materials)
- **Business Registration:** Register with Florida Department of Revenue if operating business
- **Estimated Quarterly Payments:** Only federal taxes (no state withholding)

### State Board Contact

- **Florida Department of Health, Division of Medical Quality Assurance**
- **Website:** mqa.myflorida.com (search "mental health counselor")
- **Phone/Email:** Available on website

### License Verification

Florida provides online license verification through the DBPR verification system.

### Differences from Washington

| Aspect | Washington | Florida |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **Supervised Hours Required** | 1,000 hours | 2,000 hours |
| **State Income Tax** | None | None |
| **CE Hours per 2-year period** | 20 | 20 |
| **Renewal Cycle** | Biennial (every 2 years) | Biennial |
| **Application Timeline** | 30 days before OK | 60 days before recommended |
| **Online License Lookup** | Yes | Yes |

## Multi-State Practice Considerations

### When Licensed in Both Washington and Florida

**Advantages:**
- Expand practice to serve clients in both states
- Market flexibility
- Professional networking in both communities

**Challenges:**
- Two separate renewal cycles; different deadlines
- Two separate CE requirements (40 hours total per 2-year period across both)
- Two sets of professional documents to maintain
- Higher annual licensing costs

### Strategy for Multi-State Therapists

1. **Stagger renewal dates:** If possible, schedule Washington renewal in one year and Florida in the opposite year to spread workload
   - Example: Washington expires Jan 2026, Florida expires Oct 2026
   - This prevents both renewals in the same month

2. **Unified CE tracking:** Create a single CE log that tracks hours toward both states
   - Mark which credits apply to which state
   - Most courses satisfy both states' requirements

3. **Separate renewal calendars:** Maintain state-specific reminders
   - Washington: 12 months, 6 months, 3 months, 30 days before expiration
   - Florida: 18 months, 12 months, 6 months, 60 days before expiration

4. **Document organization:** Keep state-specific folders
   - Washington renewal documents in one folder
   - Florida renewal documents in another
   - Shared: CE certificates (label which state they apply to)

## Researching Other States

### Key Information to Gather

When expanding to a new state, research:

1. **License types** and which credential is needed
2. **Education requirements** (master's vs. doctoral; approved fields)
3. **Supervised experience hours** (varies significantly by state: 1,000-2,500 hours)
4. **Exam requirements** (NCMHCE accepted in some states; others require state-specific exam)
5. **Renewal cycle** (1-year or 2-year; affects CE and cost frequency)
6. **CE requirements** (total hours, required topics, approved providers)
7. **State income tax implications** (affects estimated quarterly payments)
8. **Business registration requirements** (varies by state)
9. **Malpractice insurance licensing requirements** (some states have minimum coverage)

### Where to Research

1. **Official State Board Website**
   - Search: "[State] Department of [Health/Professional Regulation] mental health counselor"
   - Look for: License requirements, renewal information, CE guidelines, online license lookup

2. **National Counseling Association Websites**
   - American Counseling Association (ACA): counseling.org
   - National Board of Certified Counselors (NBCC): nbcc.org
   - Search for state-specific information and exam options

3. **State Licensing Board Contact**
   - Email or call to ask specific renewal questions
   - Request current renewal application (forms change; don't rely on old versions)

4. **Professional Liability Insurance Provider**
   - Verify state-specific coverage requirements
   - Some states mandate minimum coverage levels
   - Premium may vary by state

## Important Notes

- **Requirements change:** State boards update requirements regularly. Always verify with the official state board before renewal.
- **Reciprocity varies:** Some states offer reciprocity for licensed therapists from other states; most do not. Reciprocity still requires verification of credentials, usually without needing to retake exams.
- **Supervision requirements:** If you plan to supervise other therapists, additional requirements apply (varies by state).
- **Interstate practice:** Virtual therapy for out-of-state clients may have different licensing requirements; some states restrict telehealth across state lines.
